Hottest Summer Since 1954

Franklin City broke its record last month with the hottest summer since 1954, with sweltering days reaching a high of 119 degrees Fahrenheit. Experts agree, this kind of heat hasn't been seen in Franklin since 1954's June temperatures of 116 degrees Fahrenheit. Combined with this year's unusually dry spell, ranchers and farmers alike wonder what this could mean for future yields. "This year's been particularly rough," Sam Goldman, a local rancher, said last Friday. "If we've another year like this, I don't know what I'll do." Mayor Fredrich is aware of the situation and reassures Franklin inhabitants that measures will be taken if necessary. "This is alarming," Fredrich said at the last city council meeting, "but current weather figures are good. Temperatures have dropped considerably this July and there is promise of rainy showers coming in August." The people of Franklin can only hope this dry spell will soon end.

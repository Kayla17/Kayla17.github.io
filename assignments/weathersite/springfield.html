Snowstorms in May

Spring in Springfield vanished this weekend in a flurry of snow, leaving residents baffled. An estimated 6 inches fell from Friday evening to Sunday afternoon. “I’ve lived here all my life,” says 85-year-old resident, Sally Mae Johnston, “and I’ve never seen nothing like this before.” As strange as this is, expert Leon Nelson says there is probably nothing to worry about. “Cold spells like this happen every 90 years or so. Studies show it. I can almost guarantee Springfield will be back on track with normal weather patterns next spring.” In the meantime, inhabitants wonder what to do with all the snow. “The people of Springfield are having to dig out their shovels and winterwear months earlier than planned,” says mayor Cartwright. “It’s been a challenging endeavor, but we’re slowly clearing out the snow.” As strange as this all is, the children love it. “I see them [children] running to Meadow Hill with their sleds, something I never thought I’d see in May,” says Springfield elementary teacher, David Owens. While everyone pitches in to clear the snow, people of Springfield are hopeful for warmer days ahead.
